## The PAUSE Protocol

When a concerning situation arises during a session:

**P — PAUSE** the game immediately. Say: "Let's take a break from the story."

**A — ACKNOWLEDGE** what happened: "I noticed something important just came up."

**U — UNDERSTAND** — assess the situation privately if possible. Is this in-game or real?

**S — SUPPORT** the student. Address immediate needs first; story can wait indefinitely.

**E — EVALUATE** — mandatory reporter obligations, parent contact needs, documentation.

## Pennsylvania Mandated Reporter Requirements

School social workers are mandatory reporters under 23 Pa.C.S. § 6311. Suspected child abuse MUST be reported to ChildLine (1-800-932-0313) within 24 hours of reasonable suspicion forming. Do not wait for certainty. Do not investigate yourself.

## Recognizing Disclosure vs. Metaphor

Students sometimes disclose real experiences through character voices. Signs a disclosure is real:
- The student breaks character (speaks as themselves, not their character)
- The emotional intensity seems disproportionate to the game content
- The content closely mirrors the student's known life circumstances
- The student checks your reaction more than usual

When uncertain: Pause and say, "I want to make sure I understand — are you telling me something that's happening to you, or to your character?"

## Suicide/Self-Harm Disclosure

If a student discloses suicidal ideation (in-game or out-of-game):
1. PAUSE the session immediately
2. Follow your district's suicide response protocol
3. Do not leave the student alone
4. Contact school administration
5. Contact parent/guardian
6. Complete required documentation

**Never promise confidentiality before a disclosure.** Use: "I care about you and I want to help. There are some things I'd have to tell someone about to keep you safe — but I'll always tell you first."

## Documentation After a Crisis

Document same day:
- Date, time, what was said (direct quotes if possible)
- Your response
- Who you contacted and when
- Plan for follow-up
