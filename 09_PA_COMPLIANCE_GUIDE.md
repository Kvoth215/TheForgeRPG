# 🏛️ FORGE RPG Pennsylvania Compliance Guide
## Regulatory Alignment & Documentation Templates
**For PA School Social Workers, Administrators, and Grant Writers**

---

## Overview

FORGE RPG was designed from the ground up for Pennsylvania schools. This document maps FORGE to every relevant PA regulation, provides documentation templates, and gives facilitators the language they need to satisfy administrators and compliance officers.

---

## 1. ESSA Title IV-A — Student Support and Academic Enrichment

**Relevance:** Title IV-A funds "well-rounded educational opportunities," "safe and healthy students," and "effective use of technology." FORGE RPG qualifies under the **safe and healthy students** sub-grant category.

**Alignment:**
- FORGE directly targets social-emotional learning, which Title IV-A explicitly identifies as a fundable activity (§4108(5)(C)(i))
- FORGE addresses mental health (§4108(5)(C)(ii))
- FORGE supports a "positive school climate" (§4108(5)(C)(iii))

**Documentation Language:**

> *"FORGE RPG is a structured, evidence-informed small-group intervention delivered by a licensed school social worker targeting CASEL-identified social-emotional competencies including self-awareness, social awareness, and relationship skills. The program serves students at Tier 2 of the MTSS framework, providing supplemental support for students identified as at risk for social-emotional difficulties."*

---

## 2. PA Safe Schools Act (Act 26 of 1995, as amended)

**Relevance:** PA Safe Schools requires districts to address school safety through prevention programs, which explicitly includes social-emotional learning and mental health supports.

**Alignment:**
- FORGE supports social skill development that research links to reduced bullying behavior
- FORGE addresses emotional regulation, a key predictor of school violence prevention
- FORGE data (progress monitoring forms) can be included in Safe Schools plan documentation

**Safe Schools Annual Report Language:**

> *"The district implemented FORGE RPG, a therapeutic role-playing game intervention, serving [X] students in grades [X-X] during [year]. The program targeted social-emotional learning competencies aligned with the district's Safe Schools plan, with particular emphasis on peer relationship skills, emotional regulation, and constructive conflict resolution."*

## 3. Chapter 14 — Special Education

**Relevance:** Students with IEPs in the areas of social skills, emotional disturbance, or autism spectrum disorder may have FORGE RPG participation documented as a specially designed instruction (SDI) activity or as a related service supporting IEP goal progress.

### Sample IEP Goal Language

**Social Skills Domain:**

> *"When participating in structured small-group activities, [Student] will demonstrate appropriate turn-taking and active listening behaviors as measured by facilitator observation, achieving a rating of 3 or higher on the FORGE Student Growth Scale in 4 of 5 consecutive sessions by [date]."*

**Emotional Regulation Domain:**

> *"Given a frustrating or challenging situation during small-group activity, [Student] will independently use at least one identified coping strategy to maintain regulated behavior, as measured by direct observation, in 3 of 4 opportunities across 4 consecutive sessions by [date]."*

**Perspective-Taking Domain:**

> *"During structured group activities, [Student] will demonstrate awareness of peers' perspectives by accurately describing a peer's point of view in at least 2 of 3 opportunities, as measured by facilitator observation and student self-report, by [date]."*

### Progress Monitoring Notes Format

Use the FORGE Progress Monitoring Form (`tools/PROGRESS_MONITORING_FORM.md`) to generate IEP-compatible progress monitoring data. Notes should include:

- Date and session number
- Observable behaviors (specific, not interpretive)
- Performance relative to IEP goal criterion
- Supports provided
- Next steps

## 4. Chapter 16 — Gifted Education

**Relevance:** Gifted students with social-emotional needs (twice-exceptional students, gifted students with anxiety or social difficulties) can participate in FORGE RPG as part of their GIEP's affective domain support plan.

**GIEP Language:**

> *"To address [Student]'s identified need in the area of social-emotional development, [Student] will participate in FORGE RPG, a structured small-group program targeting peer relationship skills and emotional regulation, which aligns with the affective domain of [Student]'s educational needs."*

---

## 5. Chapter 49 — Educator Certification (Professional Development)

**Relevance:** School social workers who complete FORGE RPG facilitator training may count the hours toward Act 48 professional development requirements, provided training addresses PSSA/Keystone-aligned instructional topics or meets Act 48 requirements.

**Qualifying Categories:**
- Social-emotional learning instruction (qualifies under "instructional strategies")
- Trauma-informed practice training (qualifies under "student support")
- Mental health intervention training (qualifies as professional development in area of practice)

**Documentation:**
Facilitators should maintain a log of preparation time (reading facilitator guides, preparing materials) and any formal training attended. Organizations offering formal FORGE RPG training can issue Act 48 certificates.

## 6. PBIS — Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports

**Tier 2 Integration:**

FORGE RPG functions as a Tier 2 PBIS intervention. Students are referred to FORGE groups when universal (Tier 1) supports are insufficient, typically identified through:
- Office Discipline Referral data patterns suggesting social skill deficits
- Teacher nominations
- SRSS (Student Risk Screening Scale) scores
- Social skills screening tools

**FORGE Data → PBIS Data Wall:**

The FORGE Student Growth Scale can be used to populate Tier 2 data walls. Progress monitoring data should be shared with the school's PBIS team at regular intervals (recommended: monthly).

**PBIS Team Communication Template:**

> *"FORGE RPG Tier 2 Update — [Month/Year]*
> *Active participants: [X] students*
> *Average FORGE Growth Scale score: [X/4]*
> *Students demonstrating adequate progress (score ≥3): [X]*
> *Students requiring intensification (score <2 for 3+ sessions): [X]*
> *Recommended action for non-responders: [individualized plan / Tier 3 referral]*"*

## 8. FERPA and Session Note Considerations

**Key Points:**

- FORGE session notes are **educational records** under FERPA and must be protected accordingly
- If a student discloses information during a session that requires mandated reporting, follow mandated reporter protocols immediately (see `docs/08_CRISIS_RESPONSE_PROTOCOL.md`)
- Session notes documenting therapeutic conversation may, at the discretion of the school social worker, be maintained as **sole possession records** (personal working notes not shared with others, not placed in educational records, not accessible to parents)
- Data collected for IEP progress monitoring purposes **is** an educational record and must be included in the student's file

**Recommended note format:**

> *"Group session [date]. [Student] participated in [encounter]. Observed behaviors related to [IEP goal]: [specific behaviors]. Scale rating: [X/4]. No disclosures of concern. No crisis response required."*

## 9. Student Consent and Parent Notification

FORGE RPG does not require parental consent as a legal matter (it is a school-based educational activity delivered by school personnel). However, **best practice strongly recommends parental notification**, and some districts require consent for any small-group mental health activity.

Use the **Parent/Guardian Letter** (`tools/PARENT_GUARDIAN_LETTER.md`) for notification.

For districts requiring consent: Add a consent form to the parent letter. A sample consent block:

> *"I understand that my child, [Student Name], has been recommended to participate in FORGE RPG, a structured social-emotional learning program delivered by the school social worker. I understand that this program uses cooperative storytelling games to build social skills and emotional resilience. I consent to my child's participation.*
> *Parent/Guardian Signature: _________________ Date: _______"*

FORGE RPG (Fantasy Outcomes for Relationships, Growth & Empowerment) is a 
free, open-source program that uses structured cooperative storytelling to build

modules.

• Lim & Lee (2021): RCT showing 8-week TTRPG intervention significantly

  academic gains (Child Development)

  consistent positive effects (Journal of Mental Health)

• ESSA Title IV-A (Student Support and Academic Enrichment)

competencies. Data will be reported to the PBIS team monthly and summarized
