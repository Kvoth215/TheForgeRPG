# Group Composition Guide
## Clinical Guidance for Screening and Building FORGE Groups
*[Summary version — full document v1.1]*

## Who Benefits Most

FORGE RPG is designed for students who:
- Experience social skill deficits (not just behavioral problems)
- Have adequate cognitive and language skills to follow a story
- Are not in active psychosis or severe dissociation
- Have some ability to tolerate sitting with peers for 45 minutes

## Quick Screening Checklist

- [ ] Student can tolerate small group setting (3-5 peers)
- [ ] Student has basic narrative comprehension (can follow a simple story)
- [ ] Student is not in active crisis requiring immediate individual intervention
- [ ] No active peer conflict with proposed group members that poses safety risk
- [ ] Parent/guardian notification completed

## Contraindications (Clinical Caution)

- Active psychosis or significant reality testing impairment
- Severe PTSD with very low window of tolerance (individual therapy first)
- Active substance use that affects session participation
- Students currently in intense conflict with specific proposed group members (separate groups)

## Group Mixing Guidance

Mixed diagnostic groups work well and model real-world social diversity. Suggested composition for a group of 4-5:
- 1-2 students with anxiety/withdrawal presentations
- 1-2 students with social skill deficits (various causes)
- 1 student with stronger social skills (serves as a peer model — do not overload this student)
