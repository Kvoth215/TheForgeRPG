## Overview

The FORGE Student Growth Scale is a 4-point observational rubric aligned with CASEL's five competencies. Facilitators complete it every 4 sessions (Sessions 4 and 8 minimum; also baseline and 12 for 16-session programs).

## The FORGE Student Growth Scale (1–4)

**Self-Awareness**
4 = Student accurately labels own emotions and articulates their impact; connects character's experience to own
3 = Student labels emotions accurately with some prompting; occasionally makes self-connections
2 = Student identifies basic emotions with significant facilitator support
1 = Student rarely identifies or acknowledges own emotional states

**Social Awareness**
4 = Student spontaneously identifies NPC/peer emotions and considers their perspective without prompting
3 = Student identifies others' emotions with minimal prompting; attempts to consider perspective
2 = Student identifies obvious emotional cues with direct prompting
1 = Student rarely demonstrates awareness of others' emotional states

**Self-Management**
4 = Student uses coping strategies independently when dysregulated; Calm Points stay stable
3 = Student uses coping strategies with reminders; generally maintains regulation
2 = Student accepts co-regulation support; inconsistently uses strategies independently
1 = Student frequently dysregulates; strategies not yet effective

**Relationship Skills**
4 = Student initiates collaboration, uses Help Action, repairs ruptures without prompting
3 = Student collaborates consistently when included; occasionally initiates
2 = Student participates when directly invited; limited independent initiation
1 = Student resists collaboration; relationship skills in early development

**Responsible Decision-Making**
4 = Student considers consequences and others' needs before acting; learns from mistakes
3 = Student considers consequences with some prompting; generally learns from failures
2 = Student occasionally pauses to think; inconsistent learning from mistakes
1 = Student acts impulsively; difficulty connecting actions to consequences

## Using This Data

- Plot each student's scores across sessions to visualize growth
- Share with PBIS team monthly (aggregate, not individual data)
- Include in IEP progress notes (individual student data)
- Use for program evaluation and grant reporting
