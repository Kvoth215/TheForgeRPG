# 💰 FORGE RPG Grant Funding Guide
## Securing Program Funding for Pennsylvania Schools
**For Social Workers, Grant Writers & Building Administrators**

## Overview

FORGE RPG is free to download — but running it costs time, materials, and facilitator training. This guide helps you secure funding through federal, state, and private sources. Pre-written grant language is included throughout.

**Estimated annual program cost per group (3-5 students):**
| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Dice sets (reusable) | ~$15 |
| Printed character sheets, handouts | ~$10 |
| Facilitator preparation time (not budgeted separately if in SSW role) | N/A |
| Optional: minis/tokens, map materials | ~$20 |
| **Total per group** | **~$45/group** |

*This is one of the lowest-cost evidence-informed interventions available.*

## Part 1: Federal Funding Sources

### 1A. ESSA Title IV-A — Student Support and Academic Enrichment (SSAE)

**Maximum opportunity. Start here.**

Title IV-A provides formula grants to states, which distribute to LEAs. In Pennsylvania, this flows through the PDE as the SSAE subgrant. Funds can support:

- Mental health services and supports (§4108(5)(C))
- Social-emotional learning programs (§4108(5)(C)(i))
- Training for school mental health professionals (§4108(5)(C)(ii))

**Sample budget line:**

> *"FORGE RPG Therapeutic SEL Intervention — School Social Worker facilitation of 4 student groups (16–20 students), including materials, facilitator training, and program evaluation: $[X]"*

**Grant narrative paragraph (copy/adapt):**

> *"The district proposes to implement FORGE RPG, an evidence-informed therapeutic role-playing game intervention targeting social-emotional learning for Tier 2 students in grades 6–8. FORGE RPG is a free, open-source program aligned with CASEL's five SEL competencies and the SAMHSA Trauma-Informed Care framework. The program has demonstrated effectiveness in the peer-reviewed literature (Lim & Lee, 2021; Baer et al., 2022; Durlak et al., 2011) and addresses the well-established link between social-emotional skills and academic achievement. Implementation will be led by the district's licensed school social worker, serving [X] students per year in 8-session modules with CASEL-aligned progress monitoring."*

### 1B. IDEA Part B — Special Education Funds

Students with IEPs can have FORGE RPG services funded under IDEA as:
- Specially Designed Instruction (SDI) for social skills goals
- A Related Service supporting IEP implementation
- Extended School Year (ESY) programming

**Important:** Document the IEP connection carefully. The service must be tied to a specific IEP goal. Use the goal language templates in `docs/09_PA_COMPLIANCE_GUIDE.md`.

**Grant/budget language:**

> *"Social skills group intervention (FORGE RPG) for students with IEPs in emotional support and autism support programs: SDI services provided by school social worker, 45 minutes/week, 8-week modules. Service supports IEP goals in the areas of [social skills / emotional regulation / perspective-taking]."*

---

### 1C. Title I Part A — Improving Basic Programs

For schools with significant low-income populations, Title I funds can support FORGE RPG as:
- A supplemental academic support program (social-emotional skills predict academic outcomes)
- A parent engagement activity (parent orientation session)

**Evidence link to academics:**

> *"Durlak et al. (2011) found that SEL interventions produce an average 11-percentile-point gain in academic achievement. FORGE RPG's alignment with CASEL SEL competencies positions it as an academically relevant intervention eligible for Title I supplemental support funding."*

## Part 2: Pennsylvania-Specific Funding

### 2A. PA Safe Schools Grant Program

**Annual grant cycle — check PDE website for current RFP.**

Safe Schools funds prevention programs addressing school safety. FORGE RPG qualifies under the **social-emotional learning** and **mental health** prevention categories.

**Required evidence language:**

> *"FORGE RPG is grounded in the SAMHSA Trauma-Informed Care framework and the CASEL SEL model, both of which are identified by the Pennsylvania Department of Education as evidence-based approaches to school safety through prevention. The program targets Tier 2 students identified through PBIS screening as exhibiting social skill deficits associated with peer conflict, behavioral escalation, and social withdrawal."*

**Outcome measures to promise:**

- Reduction in office discipline referrals for participating students (compare pre/post)
- Improvement on FORGE Student Growth Scale (SEL progress monitoring data)
- Teacher-reported improvement in classroom social behavior
- Student self-report pre/post on FORGE SEL survey

---

### 2B. Pennsylvania Mental Health Block Grant (OMHSAS)

The Office of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services (OMHSAS) administers block grants for school-based mental health services. Contact your county's Mental Health/Intellectual Disabilities (MH/ID) office for local allocation processes.

**FORGE RPG positioning for this funder:**

> *"FORGE RPG represents an innovative, low-cost, high-engagement school-based mental health intervention for youth ages 11–15. The program uses structured cooperative role-playing games to build social-emotional skills, reduce anxiety, and improve peer relationships in a school setting. Unlike traditional group therapy, FORGE RPG's game-based format dramatically increases student engagement and reduces stigma — critical barriers to youth mental health service utilization."*

## Part 4: Grant Writing Templates

### Program Description (200 words — short grants)

> *"FORGE RPG (Fantasy Outcomes for Relationships, Growth & Empowerment) is a free, open-source therapeutic tabletop role-playing game program for middle school students. Led by a licensed school social worker, small groups of 3–5 students participate in 8-session cooperative storytelling adventures designed to build social-emotional skills including empathy, communication, and emotional regulation.*
>
> *Unlike traditional social skills curricula, FORGE RPG uses the inherently engaging format of role-playing games to motivate participation, reduce stigma, and create authentic social practice opportunities. Students play fictional characters facing social challenges that parallel real-world experiences — practicing perspective-taking, conflict resolution, and collaboration in a low-stakes imaginary context.*
>
> *The program is grounded in 40+ peer-reviewed studies on game-based therapy, narrative therapy, and social-emotional learning. All materials are openly licensed (CC BY-SA 4.0), available free of charge, and include a CASEL-aligned progress monitoring system, trauma-informed facilitation protocols, and full Pennsylvania regulatory compliance documentation."*

### Program Description (500 words — larger grants)

> *"[DISTRICT NAME] proposes to implement FORGE RPG, an evidence-informed therapeutic role-playing game intervention for students in grades [X]–[X] who demonstrate social-emotional learning needs at the Tier 2 level of the district's Multi-Tiered System of Supports (MTSS).*
>
> *FORGE RPG addresses a documented gap in school-based mental health services: the need for engaging, stigma-reducing, peer-centered interventions for early adolescents. Students ages 11–15 are in a critical developmental window for social skill formation, yet traditional group social skills programs often fail to engage this population due to developmental resistance to explicit instruction and heightened sensitivity to peer perception. FORGE RPG's game-based format sidesteps these barriers: students participate willingly in a structured social activity that feels like play while providing systematic social-emotional skill practice.*
>
> *The program's design is grounded in multiple converging evidence streams. Social-emotional learning interventions (Durlak et al., 2011; n=270,000) produce measurable academic gains alongside behavioral improvements. Game-based social interventions show specific effectiveness for peer relationship skills (Lim & Lee, 2021) and anxiety reduction (Lem & Dowling, 2021). The therapeutic distance created by character play enables deeper social-emotional processing than direct instruction (Blackmon, 1994; Malchiodi, 2011). Self-Determination Theory (Ryan & Deci, 2000) predicts that the autonomy, competence, and relatedness provided by FORGE will produce intrinsic motivation and sustained engagement.*
>
> *The program will be delivered by [Name], Licensed School Social Worker, who holds [credentials]. Groups of 3–5 students will participate in 8-session adventure modules (45 minutes/week) targeting specific CASEL competencies. The program includes a built-in CASEL-aligned progress monitoring system, trauma-informed facilitation protocols (including X-Card, Open Door, and PAUSE tools), MTSS documentation templates, and full Pennsylvania regulatory compliance materials.*
>
> *[DISTRICT NAME] will serve [X] students in [X] groups during the [year] school year, with plans for expansion to [X] groups in year 2. Program evaluation will include FORGE Student Growth Scale pre/post data, teacher perception surveys, and office discipline referral comparison data. A full program report will be produced and shared publicly under Creative Commons license.*
>
> *FORGE RPG is designed for replication and scaling. All materials are available free of charge at [GitHub URL] under a Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike license, meaning any district that adapts or improves the materials must share those improvements with the field. [DISTRICT NAME]'s implementation will contribute to a growing evidence base and practitioner community supporting game-based therapeutic intervention in schools."*

## Part 5: Common Grant Reviewer Questions

**Q: Is this evidence-based?**
> A: FORGE RPG is evidence-*informed* — grounded in 40+ peer-reviewed studies on component mechanisms (game-based therapy, narrative therapy, SEL). Direct RCT evidence on this specific program is emerging. This positions FORGE similarly to many accepted Tier 2 PBIS interventions.

**Q: Why games? Isn't this just play?**
> A: Research consistently shows that game-based formats significantly increase engagement in populations resistant to traditional interventions (Mayer, 2019; Connolly et al., 2012). The therapeutic mechanisms are well-documented — play is the delivery vehicle, not the intervention itself.

**Q: What if students disclose abuse or self-harm?**
> A: FORGE RPG includes a comprehensive Crisis Response Protocol (`docs/08_CRISIS_RESPONSE_PROTOCOL.md`) covering mandated reporting, in-session crisis response, and post-session documentation. Facilitators are licensed professionals trained in these protocols.

**Q: How will you know if it worked?**
> A: The program includes a CASEL-aligned FORGE Student Growth Scale (pre/post rubric), student self-assessment surveys, teacher perception surveys, and PBIS data comparison. Data are collected every 4 sessions and at program end.
